SECTION 6 — COLOR SYSTEM
================================================================================

--red:        #BE1E2D   Primary brand color. PS1-era red. Bold but not garish.
                         Use for: CTAs, eyebrow labels, accent lines, sticky bar,
                         stats section background, process strip link color,
                         trust bar dots, review stars, service flags.
                         Do NOT overuse. Maximum impact through restraint.

--red-dark:   #9A1724   Hover state for red elements only.

--white:      #FFFFFF   Page background default.

--snow:       #F8F7F4   Warm off-white. Use for alternating section backgrounds,
                         trust bar, service panels. Prevents visual fatigue from
                         pure white.

--ink:        #1C1B19   Near-black. All body text, nav links. Never pure #000000.
                         Also used as dark section background (process strip,
                         footer, mobile sticky email button).

--mid:        #6B6A67   Secondary text. Descriptions, metadata, timestamps.

--rule:       #E4E3DF   Borders, dividers, panel outlines. Warm toned — matches
                         the off-white palette, never cool gray.

COLOR PHILOSOPHY
The palette is intentionally minimal: red, white, and near-black. This is the
PS1-era color DNA (bold red + clean white) refined into something modern and
professional. The red carries ALL the visual weight. Every other color exists to
give the red room to breathe.

Never add new colors without owner approval. No blues, no greens, no gradients
on text. The logo taupe (#726658) appears only within the SVG itself.

SECTION 10 — COPY VOICE & TONE
================================================================================

VOICE
Direct. Confident. No filler. Sounds like the owner talking, not a marketing
department. Uses "we" — never third person ("Lunar Painting is...").

TONE BY AUDIENCE
  Property managers: Professional peer-to-peer. Speak their language.
                     ("We work around tenant schedules", "coordinate with your
                     management timeline", "minimal disruption to residents",
                     "detailed prep report for board presentations")
  HOA boards:        Formal enough for board context. Process-oriented.
                     ("within HOA color guidelines", "board-ready documentation")
  Homeowners:        Warm but never fluffy. Practical reassurance.
                     ("we don't leave until the walk-through is done")

PHRASES TO USE (from original site, use sparingly)
  - "We paint the right way."
  - "Proper application techniques to ensure a uniform finish."
  - "Get in, get the job done, clean up, and get out — without sacrificing quality."
  - "Integrity, honesty, professionalism, and attention to detail."

PHRASES TO NEVER USE
  - "Transform your space"
  - "Bring your vision to life"
  - "Our team of experts"
  - "Industry-leading"
  - "Best-in-class"
  - "Elevate your property"
  - Any sentence starting with "Whether you're looking to..."
  - Any sentence starting with "At Lunar Painting, we believe..."
  - Anything with exclamation marks in body copy

CTA COPY
  Primary:   "Call (949) 446-8578"  /  "Request a Commercial Estimate"
  Secondary: "See commercial services"  /  "See how we work"
  Never:     "Get started today!"  /  "Contact us now!"  /  "Learn more about us"

HEADLINE STYLE
  Use line breaks intentionally. Short, declarative, punchy.
  Good: "The crew / property managers / call back."
  Good: "Built for / big projects."
  Good: "Every surface. / Done properly."
  Bad:  "Orange County's Premier Full-Service Painting Contractor"
  Bad:  "Quality You Can Trust for Every Project"

SECTION 12 — INTERACTIONS & ANIMATIONS
================================================================================

WHAT IS ALLOWED
  - Scroll reveal: simple opacity + translateY via IntersectionObserver
    (.reveal class → .in class, threshold 0.1, translateY 22px → 0)
  - Staggered delays: .reveal-d1 (.1s), .reveal-d2 (.2s), .reveal-d3 (.3s)
  - Photo hover: subtle scale(1.04) on work grid images, transition .6s ease
  - Nav shadow on scroll: box-shadow added via JS class toggle on scroll > 10px
  - Button hover: background color transition to darker red (.2s ease)
  - Link hover: color + border-color transition
  - Process strip link: arrow gap increase on hover (gap animation)

WHAT IS BANNED (owner has seen these on too many Claude-built sites)
  - Marquee / ticker tape scrolling text bands
  - Text scramble / decode letter effects
  - Number color changes on hover
  - Accordion image strips / panels
  - Dark card hover reveals
  - backdrop-filter: blur() on the navbar
  - Split-column hero with geometric clip-path shapes
  - Parallax scrolling effects
  - Typewriter effects
  - Counting number animations on stats
  - Any animation that distracts from the content or feels like a demo

ANIMATION TIMING
  All transitions: cubic-bezier(.25,.46,.45,.94) — ease-out curve
  Fast interactions (buttons, links): 0.2s
  Scroll reveals: 0.75s
  Photo scale: 0.6s
  Never use linear easing except for infinite loops (which are banned anyway)

SECTION 14 — WHAT NOT TO DO (HARD RULES)
================================================================================

1.  Do not use the same layout as any other Claude-built website for this owner.
    The owner has 3+ other sites. Assume any "standard" Claude pattern is banned.

2.  Do not add rounded corners anywhere — not on buttons, cards, images, or
    containers. This brand is clean, sharp, and architectural.

3.  Do not use box-shadow on cards or panels. Use border: 1px solid var(--rule).

4.  Do not use gradients on text (no -webkit-background-clip text tricks).

5.  Do not center-align body copy. Left-aligned only.

6.  Do not put the process section as a featured home page section. It gets one
    line and a link. The full 5-step process lives on process.html.

7.  Do not use emojis in UI except the sticky mobile bar (phone/email icons).

8.  Do not add decorative SVG shapes, blobs, or wavy section dividers.

9.  Do not use any color outside the defined palette without owner approval.

10. Do not write AI-sounding copy ("Transform your space", "Our dedicated team
    of experts", "Whether you're looking to..."). Write like a real contractor.

11. Do not use <img> for the logo — always inline SVG.

12. Do not hide the phone number. It must be prominent on every page.

13. Do not add sections that aren't in the approved page structure without
    asking the owner first.

14. Do not use different fonts than Cormorant Garamond + Inter. This pairing
    was chosen deliberately to differentiate from previous sites.
